Say "Cannes" and most people think of one thing: May, the red carpet, the gowns, the Palme d'Or. Eleven days a year. The rest of the time, for people who don't work here, it's a quiet seaside town. That's wrong.

Cannes actually runs on **eight major professional gatherings a year**, and each one turns the city into the world capital of an industry in turn. If you pass through for work — or if you want to catch Cannes when it's genuinely alive — here's the calendar we've learned to read from the inside.

## March · MIPIM (real estate)

**Crowd**: developers, investors, funds, architects, elected officials.
**Energy**: grey suits, white badges, hard negotiations, €500-per-head dinners. People come to sign, not to dance.
**Afterwork**: the Martinez, the Majestic, private yachts in the old harbour. But the real *after* for decision-makers happens in members' clubs. Quieter, longer, more interesting.

MIPIM is the first big date of the year. Four days, 20,000 participants, massive deal volume signed in a handful of handshakes. The mood is tense — everyone knows the professional year is being decided here.

## April · MIPDOC + MIPTV (television)

**Crowd**: producers, distributors, channel buyers.
**Energy**: more creative and younger than MIPIM. The nights are more real.
**Afterwork**: the bars on rue du Commandant-André, hotel rooftops, independent clubs.

This is where TV formats from all over the world get bought and sold. Lots of Anglos, Latin Americans, Asians. The Croisette flips into English for a week.

## May · Cannes Film Festival

**Crowd**: world cinema, plus the entire luxury industry that piggybacks.
**Energy**: surreal. Two weeks where the city is unliveable and fascinating at the same time.
**Afterwork**: *impossible* if you're not accredited, *compulsory* if you are. Three-hour queues at the good places. Our private-hire tables are booked a year in advance.

We won't rewrite what everyone else writes. One note: the Festival exhausts the city. Local Cannois leave on holiday for those two weeks. It's *our* busiest and wildest season.

## June · Cannes Lions (advertising, creativity)

**Crowd**: agencies, brands, tech platforms, influencers.
**Energy**: the most party-driven of the eight seasons. Brand event budgets explode here.
**Afterwork**: everything, everywhere, all the time. Apple, Meta, Spotify, TikTok privatise entire beaches.

Over the last fifteen years, Cannes Lions has become a global summit of advertising and creative work. Five non-stop days during which every brand tries to stage *the* moment people will talk about. For us, it's the week we see the most interesting people — creatives, strategists, fashion-industry journalists, visual artists.

> Cannes Lions is like the Film Festival for people who work in June.

## July–August · tourist lull

**Crowd**: families, holidaymakers, boaters.
**Energy**: seaside, relaxed, sometimes empty.
**Afterwork**: the city breathes. Local clubs run at 50 % capacity but nights feel more honest, freer. Our summer programming is one of the most demanding of the year — fewer people, but those who are there *are there for the music*.

## October · MIPCOM + MIPJunior

**Crowd**: TV again, autumn-sales edition — kids formats, winter shows.
**Energy**: similar to April, quieter.

## November · Mousquetaires Trophy + Mondial du Vent

**Crowd**: two more localised sporting events bringing a specific clientele.
**Energy**: low-key but regular.

## December · NRJ Music Awards + year-end

**Crowd**: music industry for the Awards, festive tourism for year-end.
**Energy**: bright, celebratory.

The NRJ Music Awards bring the entire French pop scene onto the Croisette for 48 hours. The rest of the month, the city settles into a warm torpor — Christmas markets, family dinners, upscale New Year's Eve.

## Making sense of it all

If you're coming to Cannes for work, two rules we've watched hold true a hundred times:

1. **Book early.** During the eight big gatherings, the best tables go three to six months in advance. Good venues don't take walk-ins in those weeks.
2. **Find the *other* city.** During the Festival, interesting people flee the Croisette and gather in the alleys behind the town hall. During MIPIM, the real conversations happen in small restaurants in the Suquet, not on the Martinez terraces.

Cannes outside festival season is almost a better Cannes. Less photographed, more liveable. More real.

And the nights are just as good.
