A club bar is set up to fail an impossible equation: serve fast, serve a lot, serve well. Two out of three is easy. All three is a craft. That's why we decided in 2021 to keep only three people behind our bar — and never hire for throughput.

## What we don't do

Before we say what we do, it's more honest to say what we refuse.

**No sugary shots.** No B-52, no Jägerbomb, no tequila-rose-vodka thing. Those exist to get people drunk fast when they don't know the difference. Our job is to teach them the difference.

**No ice buckets.** A bucket on a table with a bottle of vodka is a social signal, not a tasting. We serve bottles during private hire, but always plated, crushed ice cut to order, none of those industrial cubes that dilute everything.

**No unnecessary foam.** Every year we watch trends come through — lemon foams, bitter spheres, charcoal dust. We pass. A good cocktail holds on five ingredients and one technique.

> A good cocktail is three things: a good spirit, a good balance, good timing. The rest is window dressing.

## Our three signatures

### The Glass Manhattan

Classic, to the letter. Rittenhouse 100-proof bourbon (one with backbone, not a sweet low-proof), Carpano Antica Formula vermouth, two dashes of Angostura. Stirred 45 seconds in a mixing glass, pre-chilled crystal coupe, amarena cherry garnish.

What's different: we use a high-proof bourbon so it holds up to the vermouth. Plenty of bars reach for a light bourbon so it doesn't "crush" the vermouth, and end up with a limp drink. A limp Manhattan is a signal that the bar is afraid.

### The smoked Old Fashioned

Woodford Reserve bourbon, turbinado sugar, orange bitters, flame-kissed orange peel just before service. The smoke comes from a cloche set over the glass with hickory shavings lit for ten seconds with a torch.

It's a drink that demands quiet. We serve it to people sitting down, not standing at the bar. The ceremony is part of the taste.

### The Rouge

Our house signature. A spritz that looks like nothing else on the coast. Aperol base reworked with Campari (for the bitterness), lifted with a house hibiscus cold-infused 48 hours. Valdobbiadene Prosecco from a small producer we buy direct. Served in a balloon glass with a wide grapefruit peel.

It's our bestseller. A third of the people who walk into Glass order a Rouge as their first drink. We pour about 200 a night.

## Why our bartenders take their time

An average club bartender turns out 60 cocktails an hour. Ours turn out 35. That's a choice.

- **At 60/hr**, you shake for 4 seconds, you pour without tasting, you end the night having served customers — but not delighted them.
- **At 35/hr**, you shake 12 seconds, you taste with a straw before serving, you send out a cocktail that is *exactly* the one you meant to make. The customer feels it on the first sip.

The consequence: our bar queues are sometimes longer than elsewhere. We prefer that. One customer who waits six minutes for a Manhattan that floors them is worth more than two customers who walk away in two minutes, disappointed.

## The people behind it

We won't name them because they prefer to stay off the page. But three things we can say without breaking their discretion:

Our bar lead spent three years at the Connaught in London before coming back south. She composed the menu.

Our second comes from wine — Bordeaux-trained sommelier, career switch. He brings an attention to pairings, to the terroir of spirits, to seasonality, that you don't often find behind a club bar.

Our third is the youngest — 25, self-taught, came up through specialty coffee before bartending. He runs the cleanest service on the team.

Together they took eighteen months to land the menu we serve today. Eighteen months of tasting, adjusting, subtracting, adding. A menu, here, is a slow process.

## Closing

We decided from day one to treat the bar with the same seriousness as the music. Not because we claim to rival the great cocktail bars of Paris or London — we know that isn't our primary craft. But because a club where you eat badly, drink badly and sit badly is a club you forget as soon as you walk out.

A properly built Manhattan at 1 AM marks you for a week. A shot of tequila thrown back fast leaves you with no memory.

We'd rather leave you with memories.
