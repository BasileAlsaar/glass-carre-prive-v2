You can't judge a club at first glance. The lighting lies, the décor reassures, the staff smiles. What doesn't lie is the time you leave. Six in the morning, the music still holds, you still hold — the sound is good. Four in the morning, your ears are aching, your calves are burning, you're looking for the exit — the sound is bad, no matter how brilliant the DJ was.

That difference isn't an opinion. It's a brand name: **Void Acoustics**.

## Why the sound matters first

When we started designing Glass in 2019, I toured every club I loved — Concrete in Paris before it closed, Fabric in London, De School in Amsterdam. One detail came up everywhere. When I asked what makes a club age well, the first answer was never the programming. It was the sound system.

The logic is straightforward. A line-up can change overnight. A room can be repainted. A bar can be redesigned. A sound system gets calibrated over three days by an engineer charging €8,000 and then stays in place for ten years. It's infrastructure. It's what decides, night after night, whether people stay.

> A sound system is the one thing in a club you can't fake. Everything else, you can improvise. The sound either works or it doesn't.

## What Void Acoustics is

Void is a British company founded in the late '90s by Rog Mogale, an engineer who cut his teeth at Turbosound. The first Incubus cabinets came out in 1998, with a shape you can spot from across a room — moulded fibreglass, organic, red, shaped like open shells.

That shape isn't decorative. It comes from the acoustic maths: an exponential horn with a spherical mouth gives more coherent dispersion than a rectangular wooden box. High frequencies stay sharp all the way to the edge of the floor rather than collapsing in the middle.

Today Void powers **Fabric** (London), **Printworks** (London, up until it closed in 2023), **Berghain** (side rooms), **Concrete 92** (Paris), **Club Vaag** (Antwerp). It's the global underground reference for sound that holds the distance.

## How we calibrated Glass

Our room is 90 m². An imperfect cube, low ceiling, breezeblock walls, polished concrete floor. Acoustically, it's a nightmare — endless resonances, flutter echoes in the corners, bass nulls in every angle. A mis-tuned system in there produces mud, not music.

We flew in Bastiaan, a freelance audio engineer, ex-Funktion-One, based in Amsterdam. Three days of tuning. Final setup:

- **Subs**: two Void Indigo 15s — direct-loaded, rolling off around 35 Hz. That's what hits your sternum at 2 AM.
- **Mid/high**: four Air Motion 4s in point-source, aimed per zone. No line array, no façade. Sound arrives from four directions at once — the image is wrap-around, not frontal.
- **DSP**: a Lake LM44 processor handles delays, crossovers, limiters. Calibrated to 108 dB SPL average on the floor, 112 at peak. Loud enough that the music carries, quiet enough that ears don't saturate.
- **Acoustic treatment**: absorbers behind the mirrors (added in the 2025 refurb), diffusers on the ceiling. Invisible, but that's what stops sound bouncing like a bathroom.

Result: a 3-way active system that breathes, runs deep without booming, and keeps the hi-hats sharp at 4 AM.

## What it changes for you

If you've ever danced at a club with a poorly tuned system, you know the symptoms without knowing the words:

- Fatigue that creeps in around 1–2 AM. You step outside, you chase silence. It's your ears saturating, not you getting old.
- A boom in the low-end, even when the DJ drops a break. The sub isn't aligned, bass resonates instead of striking.
- Hi-hats that vanish as the night wears on. The limiter is clamping; highs go first.

On a properly calibrated Void system, none of that. You dance three hours without noticing. You leave at 6 AM still wanting to hear one more track. That's the difference.

## The quieter story

Last year an international resident we book twice a year turned down a better-paid slot at another Cannes club to stay with us. She texted the next morning: *"I missed your system. I couldn't hear what I was mixing over there."*

That text is worth more than any marketing sheet. The sound is what makes people *who know* come back. And people who know bring the rest.

## Closing

A club that invests in a Void system isn't a club doing marketing. It's a club saying, without saying it, that it intends to stay open ten years. Because this gear doesn't pay for itself on a flashy opening — it pays for itself by being consistent, night after night, on what you want people to feel.

And what we want you to feel at Glass is the bass in your chest at 2 AM and hi-hats that are still clean at 5 AM. Not a second longer, not a second less.

The rest — lighting, décor, cocktails — is dressing. The sound is the house.
